These photos follow my visits to Montreal and Quebec City, two beautiful and historic cities in Canada. I first explored them in 2015 and returned in 2021, experiencing their charm twice.

Quebec City’s Fairmont Le Château Frontenac stands as an iconic landmark, while Montmorency Falls showcases nature’s power. Strolling through the picturesque downtown of Quebec City, witnessing streets such as Umbrella Alley, added to the city’s enchanting European feel.

Montreal, a lively city infused with French influences, impressed me with its blend of architecture and nature. The St. James United Church and the beautiful Botanical Gardens provided a mix of culture and relaxation. At the Montreal Olympic Stadium Biodome, I even encountered a varity of animal species, such as penguins.

Both visits allowed me to appreciate the rich history, breathtaking scenery, and vibrant atmosphere of these cities, making them some of my favorite travel experiences.

These photos capture my travels across Florida, a state I’ve explored throughout the years. I’ve visited Miami (2021, 2015, 2011), Naples (2021), Key West (2021), and Orlando (2015, 2014, 2013, 2011), each trip bringing its own unforgettable experiences.

Miami holds a special place in my heart, not just for its stunning beaches and vibrant nightlife but also because most of my Cuban family lives there. The city is a cultural hub, shaped by generations of Cuban immigrants who have brought their traditions, food, and music. Walking through Little Havana, sipping coffee, or hearing the lively rhythms of salsa and reggaeton, I always feel connected to my roots.

Orlando was a big part of my childhood, with multiple visits to Disneyworld and Universal Studios, where I made incredible memories with my cousins—posing with Pluto, exploring Cars Land, and taking in the magical atmosphere.

In Key West (2021), I stood at the iconic Southernmost Point, just 90 miles from Cuba— a powerful reminder of my heritage. Naples (2021), on the other hand, was all about relaxation, ending my day with a breathtaking sunset at the pier.

From family reunions in Miami to thrilling theme park adventures in Orlando, my Florida trips have always been filled with nostalgia, culture, and fun.

These photos capture the highlights of my trips to Cancún (2018, 2016) and Riviera Maya (2013, 2012)—destinations that offer the perfect mix of stunning beaches, Mayan history, and thrilling adventures. Whether relaxing on the soft sands of Cancún’s world famous coastline or exploring adrenaline pumping eco parks, every trip was filled with unforgettable experiences.

The beaches of Cancún and Riviera Maya are truly spectacular, with crystal clear turquoise waters, powdery white sand, and breathtaking sunsets. Some of my best memories were spent relaxing by the shore, swimming in the warm Caribbean Sea, and enjoying beachfront resorts with endless ocean views. The water was perfect for snorkeling, where I saw colorful fish and coral reefs. Cancún’s vibrant hotel zone was ideal for lively beach days, while Riviera Maya had more secluded spots, offering a peaceful escape into nature.

My most favorite experiences came from the thrilling and action packed adventure parks and excursions. Here are some of my favourites that I have visited:

Xel-Há: The Ultimate Natural Water Park

At Xel-Há, I experienced a natural aquatic paradise where I snorkeled alongside tropical fish in cenotes and swam in a massive open sea inlet. Floating down the lazy river in an inner tube, surrounded by lush jungle, was one of the most relaxing experiences. For adventure, I tried cliff jumping, zip lines that land in the water, and even biking through jungle trails.

Xenses: A Mind Bending Adventure

Visiting Xenses was unlike anything I’d ever done—it’s a sensory illusion park that plays with perception. I walked through a town where gravity was reversed, floated effortlessly in a salt river, and entered a pitch dark cave that heightened my other senses. The Xensatorium simulated different ecosystems using only sound and touch, making it a surreal experience.

Xplor: The Ultimate Adventure Park

Xplor was pure adrenaline, featuring the longest zip lines in Riviera Maya, which soared above the jungle with breathtaking views. I also drove an amphibious vehicle through caves and water filled trails, swam in underground rivers illuminated by stalactites, and rafted through subterranean caves. The park’s torch lit underground rivers made it feel like an Indiana Jones adventure.

Selvatica: The Jungle Adventure

At Selvatica, I experienced one of the most thrilling zip-lining circuits in Mexico, flying through the jungle canopy. After zip-lining, I took an ATV ride through muddy jungle trails and ended the adventure by jumping into a cenote, a deep natural sinkhole filled with refreshing blue water.

Between the beaches, adventure parks, and cultural experiences, my trips to Cancún and Riviera Maya had the perfect mix of relaxation and excitement. Whether I was floating in the sea, zip-lining over the jungle, or exploring underground rivers, Mexico always offered something new and unforgettable.
