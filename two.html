Segovia

Across two trips to Spain—one in 2022 and another in 2024—I had the chance to explore a wide range of cities, each with its own vibe, history, and highlights. From big, iconic cities like Madrid and Barcelona to historic gems like Toledo and Segovia, every place had something memorable to offer.

In Madrid, I visited the famous art museums, walked through the grand Royal Palace, and spent time wandering through Retiro Park. The mix of culture, architecture, and daily street life made the capital feel alive at every hour.

Barcelona was all about views and creativity. From the stunning Sagrada Família to the scenic overlook at Montserrat, and the coastal charm of nearby Tossa de Mar, it felt like a mix of modern energy and artistic heritage.

Sevilla gave me a more traditional Andalusian experience—flamenco dancing in open squares, bright alleyways, and iconic landmarks like Plaza de España and the Real Alcázar. In Granada, the Alhambra was unforgettable. Its detailed Islamic architecture and hillside views made it one of the most beautiful places I’ve ever seen.

Toledo and Segovia had a completely different feel. With their medieval architecture, cathedrals, and castles, they felt like stepping into a different century. Walking through stone streets with centuries old buildings surrounding me was surreal.

Malaga and Mallorca gave me the beach moments—swimming in the Mediterranean, exploring sea caves, and just enjoying the coast. In Mallorca, I especially loved the mix of mountain and beach landscapes—it felt peaceful but adventurous at the same time.

Both trips gave me a deep appreciation for Spanish culture, food, and history. Whether I was trying tapas in a busy square, climbing steps to a cathedral, or just enjoying the view from a cliffside trail, Spain always had something new to offer. I’d go back in a heartbeat.

Florence

In 2023, I traveled across Italy, visiting some of the country’s most iconic cities and coastlines. The trip had a bit of everything—history, art, food, and stunning views from both land and sea.

Rome was packed with legendary landmarks. I explored St. Peter’s Basilica, stood by the glowing Colosseum at night, and tossed a coin into the Trevi Fountain. The city felt like walking through a giant open air museum, where every street had a story.

Venice was completely different—quiet canals, colorful buildings, and no cars in sight. Riding a gondola was a bucket list moment, and wandering through Burano with its bright houses was like stepping into a painting.

I also spent time in Lake Como, which was a highlight. The scenery there was unreal—calm blue water surrounded by mountains and charming lakeside towns. I got to swim, explore villas, and catch some incredible sunsets.

Genoa was a cool surprise. I walked around the harbor, visited an aquarium, and saw some of the city's old world charm mixed with modern coastal life. Then came Portofino, which was small but gorgeous, with colorful buildings, fancy yachts, and postcard worthy views.

Finally, I visited Florence, where the art and architecture were on another level. The Duomo was even more impressive in person, and seeing the Leaning Tower of Pisa was a fun day trip. One of the more playful moments was visiting a trick eye museum where everything looked upside down.

Each city had its own personality, and what made this trip stand out was the variety—from ancient ruins to luxury coastlines to peaceful lakeside towns. It felt like I got to see a little bit of everything Italy has to offer.

In 2024, I spent time in Paris and Versailles, and it was one of the most beautiful and photo worthy trips I’ve taken. Paris truly lives up to its reputation—there’s something special about walking through streets lined with classic architecture, cafés, and monuments that you’ve seen a hundred times in photos.

I visited some of the most iconic places in the city, like the Arc de Triomphe, the Eiffel Tower, and Montmartre. Seeing them up close made me appreciate how much history and detail is built into every structure. I also spent time exploring inside the Palace of Versailles, including its famous Hall of Mirrors and massive gardens, which were even more impressive in person.

This trip gave me a real appreciation for French art, history, and design. Every corner had a story behind it, and I’d definitely go back to see even more.
