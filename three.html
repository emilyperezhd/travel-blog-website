Costa Rica

Looking out over La Fortuna Waterfall

Ziplining with Volcano Behind

Visiting Rio Celeste Waterfall

Crossing a Suspension Bridge

In 2019, I went to Costa Rica, and it was completely different from any other trip I’ve taken. We stayed in La Fortuna, a town surrounded by rainforest, volcanoes, and rivers—and spent the entire time in the jungle.

Instead of beaches or city tours, every day was packed with outdoor adventure. We went on long hikes through the rainforest, crossed suspension bridges, and saw wildlife like sloths, snakes, and colorful birds. We visited hot springs heated by Arenal Volcano, went ziplining high above the trees, and even tried bungee jumping.

One of the highlights was hiking to bright blue rivers and climbing up waterfalls—including the famous Rio Celeste, which looks unreal in person. It was the most active trip I’ve ever done, and every day felt like a new challenge or discovery.

Cuba

Celebrating my 4th Birthday

Pigeons in the Havana City Centre

Dolphin at Acuario Nacional

These photos represent Cuba, a place that is deeply connected to my identity and family history. My entire background is Cuban—all of my grandparents and most of my cousins still live there. My parents immigrated to Canada in 2003, but I grew up visiting Cuba almost every year until 2015, when we started bringing my family from Cuba to visit us in Canada instead.

Every visit to Cuba was more than just a trip—it was about family reunions, reconnecting with my roots, and immersing myself in the Cuban way of life. From the warmth of my relatives to the vibrant energy of Havana’s streets, every moment felt like home. The country’s rich culture, lively music, and strong sense of community have shaped my identity.

Cuba is home to some of the most beautiful beaches in the world, with crystal clear turquoise waters and soft white sand. One of my favorite memories was paddleboarding with family, enjoying the peaceful ocean while surrounded by breathtaking views. The beach was always a place for relaxation, fun, and making memories with my loved ones.

Even though I no longer visit Cuba as often, it remains a huge part of who I am. My family, traditions, and childhood memories are all deeply rooted there. Whether it was playing on the beach, running through Havana’s plazas, or celebrating milestones with loved ones, Cuba will always be a place that feels like home to me.

Aruba

Wild Donkeys on the Street

Jet Skiing on the Beach

Parasailing over the Clear Waters

Snorkeling with a Variety of Sealife

In 2019, I visited Aruba, a stunning Caribbean island known for its white sandy beaches, turquoise waters, and exciting outdoor activities. This trip was a perfect mix of adventure and relaxation, with plenty of opportunities to explore both land and sea.

One of the highlights was encountering wild donkeys, a unique part of Aruba’s landscape. The island’s clear waters made it ideal for snorkeling, where I swam among colorful fish and vibrant marine life. For a more thrilling experience, I went jet skiing, racing along the coastline, and parasailing, enjoying breathtaking views of the island from above.

Aruba’s natural beauty and variety of activities made it an unforgettable destination. From water sports to wildlife encounters, every moment on the island was filled with adventure and incredible scenery.

Dominican Republic

Walking the Ocean Floor

Tubing Ride Attached to Boat

Cherishing Moments with Family

I’ve been to the Dominican Republic (specifically Punta Cana) twice — once in 2010, and again in 2017. Both trips were really different, but each one had its highlights.

In 2010, I went with my family and did some fun, touristy stuff. I got my hair braided, which felt like a must do at the time. We also visited a spot where you could interact with animals—I got to hold a monkey and even a toucan. That trip was more about exploring and trying out new things with my family.

The photos here are from 2017, which was more beach and activity focused. I went helmet diving for the first time, which was a cool experience walking along the ocean floor surrounded by fish. We also went tubing behind a boat, hung out on the beach, and just enjoyed the sun. It was a fun, relaxed trip with lots of time in and around the water.

Both trips were super memorable in their own way. The first one was more about discovery, and the second was more about fun and soaking it all in.
